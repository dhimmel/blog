The impending data copyrightmare

January 11, 2016

For the last six months, I’ve been living a copyrightmare. I’ve learned that vast majority of available data is not usable data. Due to copyright law most research data that is made public is unsuitable for reuse. Accordingly, many scientists are unknowing infringers.

Authors of original works are automatically granted copyright, which provides an exclusive right to copy, reproduce, distribute, or create derivates. Currently in the U.S., copyright lasts until all authors die plus 70 years, at which point the work passes into the public domain.
